▶ Methodology & limitations

Tubing calculation: Tubing volume is calculated from inner diameter and length using V(mL) = (π/4) × ID(mm)2 × Length(m) × Quantity.

Builder total: Added items are summed to estimate total circuit prime volume.

Real-world variation: Actual circuit volume can vary due to connectors, stopcocks, bridge lines, transducers, clamps, manufacturing tolerance, and local setup.

Oxygenator presets: Preset values must be verified with the manufacturer IFU and the exact local product configuration.

Reservoir volume: Use the intended operating or fill volume, not maximum reservoir capacity.

Hemodilution prediction: Use the volume that will effectively mix with the patient’s circulating blood.

Clinical use: This tool is for educational and setup-planning purposes and does not replace institutional protocols.
