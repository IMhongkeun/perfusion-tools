Clinical context

• Target ranges: Many adult programs maintain approximately 21–30% on pump, with higher goals in pediatric and neonatal patients.

• Practical levers: Lower prime volume and UF tend to raise Hct, while crystalloid additions lower Hct.

• Transfusion planning: RBC unit count, unit volume, and unit Hct assumptions directly shift predicted output.

Educational support tool only; verify with measured laboratory values.

▶ Methodology & limitations

EBV model: EBV (mL) = Weight (kg) × EBV coefficient (mL/kg).

Red cell volume: RBC_patient = EBV × (Pre-CPB Hct ÷ 100), and RBC_products = (Units × Volume per unit) × (Unit Hct ÷ 100).

Total volume: V_total = EBV + Prime + Additional fluids + RBC product volume − UF removed.

Predicted Hct: Predicted Hct (%) = (RBC_patient + RBC_products) ÷ V_total × 100.

Limitations: Assumes complete mixing and does not fully model rapid bleeding/reinfusion shifts or compartment changes. Use with clinical markers and local protocol.
